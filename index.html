Taroko National Park

Taroko National Park is one of the nine national parks in Taiwan. It is situated in the eastern part of Taiwan and covers more than 92,000 hectares in the northern section of the Central Mountain Range. This park features high mountains and sheer gorges. Many of its peaks tower above 3,000m in elevation, with many natural wonders.

History

  • 1937 - originally established as the Tsugitaka-Taroko National Park when Taiwan was part of the Empire of Japan
  • 1945 - abolished by the Rupublic of China after World War II
  • 1986 - reestablished by the government

Location

The park spans Taichung Municipality, Nantou County, and Hualien County and is located in Xiulin Township, Hualien County, Taiwan.

Sights to See

  • Tunnel of Nine Turns
  • Eternal Spring Shrine
  • Yenzikou, Swallow Grotto Trail
  • Jinheng Park
  • Cimu Bridge, Motherly Devotion Bridge
  • Zhuilu Cliff
  • Qingshui Cliffs
